Local Food in Bandung

Bandung is a culinary haven known for its diverse food scene, from savory street snacks to flavorful traditional dishes. The city offers a unique blend of Sundanese and modern flavors that have gained popularity not only across Indonesia but also internationally. Below are some of the most popular and iconic dishes from Bandung that you must try.

Batagor

Batagor, or Bakso Tahu Goreng, is a fried fish dumpling served with a savory peanut sauce. It is one of Bandung's most famous street foods, loved for its crispy texture and rich, flavorful sauce.

Mie Kocok Bandung

Mie Kocok Bandung is a noodle dish served in a savory beef broth, topped with beef tendon and bean sprouts. The dish gets its name from the shaking (or "kocok") process used to mix the noodles before serving.

Personal Rating:

Seblak

Seblak is a spicy noodle dish made with crackers, vegetables, and proteins such as eggs or chicken. Known for its bold flavors and spice, Seblak is a favorite among Bandung locals who enjoy a strong kick in their food.

Personal Rating:

Lotek and Karedok

Lotek and Karedok are Sundanese vegetable dishes served with a rich peanut sauce. While Lotek is cooked and served warm, Karedok is a raw salad. Both offer a refreshing mix of fresh vegetables, making them a healthy and tasty choice.

Personal Rating:

Colenak

Colenak is a traditional Bandung dessert made from grilled fermented cassava, topped with brown sugar and shredded coconut. Its name comes from "dicocol enak," meaning "dipped and delicious."

Personal Rating:

Bandros

Bandros is a traditional coconut pancake, soft inside with a slightly crispy edge. Often served with sugar or savory toppings, this is a favorite snack for both locals and tourists alike.

Personal Rating:

Combro

Combro is a deep-fried snack made from grated cassava stuffed with spicy oncom (fermented soybean). It is crispy on the outside with a savory, spicy filling on the inside, making it a popular street food.

Personal Rating:

Misro

Misro, meaning "sweet inside," is similar to Combro but filled with sweetened coconut. The combination of crispy fried cassava and sweet coconut filling makes it a favorite snack among Bandung locals.

Personal Rating:

Cuanki

Cuanki is a type of fish ball soup commonly sold by street vendors in Bandung. The fish balls are served in a clear broth, often accompanied by tofu, dumplings, and crackers.

Personal Rating:

Gehu Jeletot

Gehu Jeletot is a spicy fried tofu snack filled with vegetables and chili. It is famous for its extreme heat, and the "jeletot" refers to the strong, fiery sensation it provides when eaten.

Personal Rating:

Cimol

Cimol are deep-fried tapioca balls, crunchy on the outside and chewy on the inside, often served with a variety of seasonings such as chili powder, cheese powder, or seaweed.

Personal Rating:

Lumpia Basah

Lumpia Basah is a fresh spring roll filled with bamboo shoots, bean sprouts, and egg. It is typically served with a sweet, tangy sauce and is a popular snack in Bandung's street food markets.

Personal Rating:

Peuyeum

Peuyeum is fermented cassava, known for its sweet and tangy flavor. It is often enjoyed as a dessert or snack and is a famous souvenir for visitors to take home from Bandung.
